4. Key Opportunities

Three highest-leverage areas for the next period:

1Improve mid-funnel engagement
Engagement rate dropped 2.1 pp as top-of-funnel volume increased. This is a natural tension point — more cold traffic entering the funnel means the average engagement rate drops. The fix is better content-to-audience matching: segment messaging by awareness stage, and add a dedicated nurture track for new leads before they hit conversion-oriented touchpoints.
Goal: Restore engagement rate to 32%+ within 60 days.
2Activate the referral program at scale
The referral program converts well (8.55 rev/unit) but only reaches 620 interactions per period. The current flow relies on passive opt-in. We recommend an active post-purchase prompt: at the moment of highest satisfaction (immediately after first successful outcome), present a specific referral incentive. This alone could 3x activation rate without changing the underlying offer.
Goal: +150% referral interactions within one quarter.
3Address rising churn before it compounds
Churn at 4.2% is marginally above the 4% benchmark. At current scale this costs approximately $3,500/month in lost recurring revenue. Early intervention is cheap. We'll implement a risk-signal trigger: customers who show reduced activity after 30 days enter a targeted win-back sequence with a clear value reminder and a low-friction re-engagement offer.
Goal: Return churn rate to below 3.5% within 90 days.

5. Summary

Where we stand and what comes next

Performance is on a solid trajectory. Revenue is growing, conversion rates are within target range, and the core acquisition engine is healthy. The infrastructure is in place and delivering results.


The gaps are defined and addressable — engagement rate, referral volume, and early-stage churn. None of these are structural problems; they are optimization levers with clear action plans. We know exactly what to do in the next 90 days.
