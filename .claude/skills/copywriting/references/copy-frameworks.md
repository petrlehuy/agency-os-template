# Copy Frameworks Reference

## Headline Formulas

### Outcome-Focused
- "{Achieve desirable outcome} without {pain point}" — e.g. "Understand how users are really experiencing your site without drowning in numbers"
- "{Achieve desirable outcome} by {how product makes it possible}" — e.g. "Generate more leads by seeing which companies visit your site"
- "Turn {input} into {outcome}" — e.g. "Turn your hard-earned sales into repeat customers"
- "[Achieve outcome] in [timeframe]" — e.g. "Get your tax refund in 10 days"

### Problem-Focused
- "Never {unpleasant event} again" — e.g. "Never miss a sales opportunity again"
- "{Question highlighting the main pain point}" — e.g. "Hate returning stuff to Amazon?"
- "Stop [pain]. Start [pleasure]." — e.g. "Stop chasing invoices. Start getting paid on time."

### Audience-Focused
- "{Key feature/product type} for {target audience}" — e.g. "Advanced analytics for Shopify e-commerce"
- "{Key feature/product type} for {target audience} to {what it's used for}" — e.g. "An online whiteboard for teams to ideate and brainstorm together"
- "You don't have to {skills or resources} to {achieve desirable outcome}" — e.g. "With Ahrefs, you don't have to be an SEO pro to rank higher"

### Differentiation-Focused
- "The {opposite of usual process} way to {achieve desirable outcome}" — e.g. "The easiest way to turn your passion into income"
- "The [category] that [key differentiator]" — e.g. "The CRM that updates itself"

### Proof-Focused
- "[Number] [people] use [product] to [outcome]" — e.g. "50,000 marketers use Drip to send better emails"
- "{Key benefit of your product}" — e.g. "Sound clear in online meetings"

### Additional Formulas
- "The simple way to {outcome}"
- "Finally, {category} that {benefit}"
- "{Outcome} without {common pain}"
- "Get {benefit} from your {thing}"
- "{Action verb} your {thing} like {admirable example}"
- "What if you could {desirable outcome}?"
- "Everything you need to {outcome}"
- "The {adjective} {category} built for {audience}"

## Landing Page Section Types

### Core Sections

**Hero (Above the Fold)**
- Headline + subheadline
- Primary CTA
- Supporting visual
- Optional: Social proof bar

**Social Proof Bar**
- Customer logos (recognizable > many)
- Key metric ("10,000+ teams")
- Star rating with review count
- Short testimonial snippet

**Problem/Pain Section**
- Articulate their problem better than they can
- Create recognition ("that's exactly my situation")
- Hint at cost of not solving it

**Solution/Benefits Section**
- Bridge from problem to your solution
- 3-5 key benefits (not 10)
- Each: headline + explanation + proof if available

**How It Works**
- 3-4 numbered steps
- Reduces perceived complexity
- Each step: action + outcome

**Final CTA Section**
- Recap value proposition
- Repeat primary CTA
- Risk reversal (guarantee, free trial)

### Supporting Sections

**Testimonials** — Full quotes, names, roles, companies, photos, specific results
**Case Studies** — Problem → Solution → Results, specific metrics
**Use Cases** — Different ways product is used, helps visitors self-identify
**Personas / "Built For" Sections** — "Perfect for [role]" blocks
**FAQ Section** — Address common objections, good for SEO, 5-10 questions
**Comparison Section** — vs. competitors or status quo
**Integrations / Partners** — Logos, "Works with your stack"
**Founder Story / Manifesto** — Why you built this, emotional connection
**Demo / Product Tour** — Interactive demos, video, GIFs
**Pricing Preview** — Teaser, starting price
**Guarantee / Risk Reversal** — Money-back, free trial, "Cancel anytime"
**Stats Section** — Key credibility metrics

## Page Structure Templates

### Feature-Heavy Page (Weak)
1. Hero → Feature 1 → Feature 2 → Feature 3 → Feature 4 → CTA
*Assessment: A list, not a persuasive narrative.*

### Varied, Engaging Page (Strong)
1. Hero with clear value prop
2. Social proof bar
3. Problem/pain section
4. How it works (3 steps)
5. Key benefits (2-3)
6. Testimonial
7. Use cases or personas
8. Comparison to alternatives
9. Case study snippet
10. FAQ
11. Final CTA with guarantee
*Assessment: Tells a story and addresses objections.*

### Compact Landing Page
1. Hero → Social proof bar → 3 key benefits with icons → Testimonial → How it works → Final CTA with guarantee
*Best for: Ad landing pages.*

### Enterprise/B2B Landing Page
1. Hero → Logo bar → Problem section → Solution overview → Use cases by role → Security/compliance → Integration logos → Case study with metrics → ROI/value section → Contact/demo CTA
*Best for: Enterprise buyer concerns.*

### Product Launch Page
1. Hero with launch announcement → Video demo → Feature highlights (3-5) → Before/after comparison → Early testimonials → Launch pricing → CTA with urgency
*Best for: ProductHunt, launches, announcements.*

## Section Writing Tips

### Problem Section Starting Phrases
- "You know the feeling..."
- "If you're like most [role]..."
- "Every day, [audience] struggles with..."
- "We've all been there..."

Include: specific frustration, time/money wasted, impact on work/life.

### Benefits Section
For each benefit — Headline (the outcome) + Body (how it works, 1-2 sentences) + Proof (number, testimonial, or example, optional).

### How It Works Section
Each step: numbered + simple verb ("Connect", "Set up", "Get") + outcome-oriented.
Example: 1. Connect your tools (takes 2 minutes) → 2. Set your preferences → 3. Get automated reports every Monday

### Testimonial Selection
Strongest: specific results ("increased conversions by 32%"), before/after context, role + company, something quotable.
Avoid: "Great product!", "Love it!", "Easy to use!"
